# Time Allocation

Use this file to allocate time across narrative sections and estimate
slide count for the handoff to `simple-sci-ppt`.

## Duration-Based Budget

| Talk duration | Content slides (approx) | Q&A time | Transition buffer |
|---|---|---|---|
| 10 min | 6-8 | 2-3 min | 1 min |
| 15 min | 10-12 | 3-5 min | 1-2 min |
| 20 min | 14-16 | 5 min | 1-2 min |
| 30 min | 18-22 | 5-8 min | 2 min |
| 45 min | 25-30 | 8-10 min | 2-3 min |

These are guidelines, not hard limits. A data-heavy slide may need 2-3
minutes; a transition slide may need 30 seconds.

## Section Budget

For each section in the narrative arc, assign:

| Section | Purpose | Time (min) | Approx slides | Density |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `<section name>` | `<why this section exists>` | `<min>` | `<count>` | light / medium / heavy |

Rules:

- The climax section should receive the most time and the strongest
  visual evidence.
- Background should be concise. A common failure is spending 40% of the
  talk on background.
- Reserve at least 1 minute for the conclusion section.
- Do not allocate more than 5 minutes to any single section unless it
  is the climax.
- If the total exceeds the talk duration, cut or compress before
  proceeding to slide-level planning.

## Slide Count Estimation

For the handoff to `simple-sci-ppt`, provide:

- **Total approximate slides**: cover + outline + content + conclusion.
- **Per-section slide estimate**: how many slides each section needs.
- **Density note**: which sections are figure-heavy (need more visual
  slots) and which are formula-heavy (need more card space).

## Overflow Plan

If the planned content slightly exceeds the time budget:

1. Identify the lowest-priority section.
2. Compress it into fewer slides or move it to a backup section.
3. Record the compression in `talk_plan.md`.
4. If the overflow is more than 20%, recommend cutting a takeaway or
   reducing scope.

## Rules

- Do not plan more slides than the audience can absorb. A 15-minute
  talk with 25 slides will be rushed and unreadable.
- Reserve transition time between sections. Two adjacent sections with
  different visual styles need a brief verbal bridge.
- If the talk is a poster presentation or a lightning talk (5 min),
  reduce to 3-4 content slides with a single strong claim.
- The time budget is a constraint on the narrative arc, not a suggestion.
  If the arc does not fit, revise the arc.
